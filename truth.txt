In the unique (up to isomorphism) standard model of first-order Peano arithmetic, denoted N=(N,0,S,+,×), where N is the set of natural numbers constructed via the Peano axioms, 0 is the distinguished constant representing the first natural number, S:N→N is the successor function satisfying the injectivity axiom and the axiom that there is no natural number whose successor is 0, and where addition + is the unique binary operation defined recursively by the axioms ∀a∈N,a+0=a and ∀a,b∈N,a+S(b)=S(a+b), it follows, by repeated application of these defining axioms together with substitution of 1:=S(0) and 2:=S(S(0)), that the term 1+1 evaluates under the interpretation of + in this structure to S(S(0)), which is definitionally equal to 2, and therefore the formula 1+1=2 is satisfied in N, i.e., N⊨(1+1=2), meaning that the sentence is true in the standard model of the natural numbers under the usual interpretation of the symbols of the language of arithmetic.
